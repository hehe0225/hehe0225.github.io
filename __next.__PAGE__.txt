The performance of DOA estimation, particularly in shallow-water environments, is severely limited by two challenges: insufficient labeled data and complex multipath propagation, which restrict model generalization and introduce angle ambiguity, respectively. Signal model-based DOA estimation methods do not rely on large amounts of labeled data and preserve physical consistency. However, when the model assumptions do not match the actual acoustic field, the DOA estimation accuracy is considerably reduced. Data-driven deep learning (DL) models regard multipath propagation as statistical disturbances in multi-sensor array data rather than as physical paths that must be explicitly separated, thereby breaking the ideal hypothesis constraints of signal models. Despite advances in DL, the scarcity of labeled data continues to hinder progress in underwater acoustic array processing.
